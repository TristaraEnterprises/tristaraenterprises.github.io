Graduate Student Application Process

Deadlines for applications for fall admission to graduate programs (the most common start time) are usually in January or February of that year, and sometimes even in November or December of the preceeding year. For international students especially, with extra paperwork required, it is important to begin the application process as early as possible, in most cases in the early fall of your senior year as an undergraduate student.

Since graduate programs vary, and requirements for masters and Ph.D. programs vary, each program will require specific information. However, there are some general trends. You will need to complete an online application, send transcripts and recommendation letters, take the TOEFL, and submit bank statements proving your family can afford the tuition. You will likely also have to write one or more essays, submit the titles of past scholarly or published work, explain your future research plans, take standardized tests such as the GRE, or have an interview.
