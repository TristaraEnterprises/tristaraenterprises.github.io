Transfer Student Frequently Asked Questions

Question:

Will I have to take classes or a year of school over again?

Answer:

It's possible. When you transfer, the American university will require that you get your previous college credits evaluated by an international association. Based on the results, they may give you equivalent credit at their university.

If they determine that the credits are not equivalent, you will need to retake some classes at the American university. Depending on how many classes you need to retake, you may have to take a semester or year over again.
