Transfer Student

If you have attended some college in your home country, then you can apply to universities in the United States as a transfer student. Most transfer student application deadlines are in February or March for the following Fall Term, so as an international student, you should begin the application process in the fall about one year before you hope to transfer.

When you transfer, you can try to transfer your previous credits. American Universities require that your credtis be evaluated by an independent body to see if they are equivalent to the credits offered at their own institution. In some cases, all of your credits may not transfer and you may have to retake a class in America that you already took in your home country. In addition, while you may have been a junior or senior in your home institution, after you transfer, you may only be considered a sophomore or junior. Therefore, transferring schools internationally usually means that you cannot finish your entire degree (begun in your home country) within four years.
